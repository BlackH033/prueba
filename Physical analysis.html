SOIL PHYSICAL ANALYSIS




Soil physical analysis is a very important tool to evaluate the behavior of air and water in the soil, the rooting space and some aspects related to the nutrient elements regime. It is a complement to the evaluation of chemical-nutrient analyses. The uptake of water and nutrients by plants depends fundamentally on physical properties such as texture, soil structure and moisture content. Moisture content is variable depending on the amount of sand, silt and clay it contains (texture) and the density with which these particles are structured (bulk density). The objective of this workshop is to learn the methodologies to perform a physical soil analysis under safe conditions and with acceptable and reliable results. Documents on basic soil concepts will be read: texture, bulk density and moisture content. Images of laboratory work will be observed, real data will be provided and soil texture, bulk density and moisture retention curve will be calculated.
